# Editor's Pass on the Log, June 2026

Notes captured from an editor reviewing the log posts. A review, not a rewrite. Suggestions you can take or leave. The reviewer kept Sean's voice, dry humor, and unfinished edges intact on purpose. Where the notes push, they push toward focus and gravity, not polish.

Working from the post content in the project plus the "Complete List" doc; the live page wasn't pulled. If the live set differs, say so and the reviewer will redo.

## The one pattern worth fixing everywhere

Most of these read as logbooks: 7/5, 7/6, 7/7. A real form with its charms, but it buries the thing the piece is actually about. Almost every entry would land harder with one short framing paragraph at the top that names the real subject, then lets the day-by-day run underneath unchanged. The true subject is usually found mid-way through writing. The fix is to move that discovery up to the front once it's found.

Second pattern: nearly every post wants exactly one image to anchor it, and a few want a small diagram more than a photo. The hand-drawn SVG route maps from the sailing page are the best asset here. Pulling them into the matching log entry would tie the two pages together and do more than any stock photo.

Third: the humor is the voice. "The best worst idea ever," "we got drunk instead," "stay far, far away, there's nothing to see up there." Protect those. They are doing more work than you think.
