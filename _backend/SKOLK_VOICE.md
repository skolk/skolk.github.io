## How he writes (the voice to keep)

- **Honest, specific, slightly understated.** No hype. Shows the work, not just the conclusion.
- **Concrete over abstract.** He reaches for real nouns and numbers: *$8,000*, *14 other builders*,
  *40+ people*, *Seymour Narrows*, *a drywall mounter*. Keep the specifics; they are the voice.
- **Wry, self-aware asides.** "I built a tiny house on the tiny house." "Living in your construction
  project: turns out this is a bad idea." Keep the dry humor; don't sand it off.
- **Systems thinking, plainly stated.** "The host role is the load-bearing variable." "The repetition
  is the work." He finds the structural truth and says it without jargon.
- **Logbook with a thesis.** His instinct is day-by-day chronology. The move toward broad appeal (the
  100r / craigmod bar) is a framing paragraph up top that names the real subject, so the log becomes
  evidence for a point. When editing, add or surface that framing; don't bury it.
- **Comma for the pause, not the em-dash.** Period to end a thought. Colon for label-then-description.

## Voice-to-text: how to handle his raw input

Most of what Sean sends is dictated while walking. Expect homophones (*sevearl, priotize, apeal,
wich, refrence, tlaking*), dropped apostrophes, run-on sentences, lowercase starts, and phonetic
spellings. **Read through the artifacts to intent.** When converting a transcript into a piece:

- **Clean the mechanics** (spelling, punctuation, sentence boundaries) silently.
- **Keep his actual words and rhythm.** Do not paraphrase him into generic blog voice. If he said
  "nouns and verbs on a boat," that phrase stays; it is better than anything smoother.
- **Do not add what he didn't say.** No invented detail, no filler transitions, no motivational
  wrap-up. When unsure of a fact he garbled, flag it for him rather than guessing.

## Banned / avoid (LLM-puff, the R-007 risk)

Never let these into a draft: *delve, tapestry, testament to, in today's fast-paced world, navigate
the complexities, unlock, leverage (as filler), embark on a journey, it's important to note*, hollow
symmetry, and three-adjective lists that say nothing. If a sentence could appear on any blog, cut it.
The test: would Sean, specifically, say this out loud on a walk?
