New 14er. I decided to wake up at 2am and drive to the Longs Peak trailhead, then hike three hours in the dark alone until the sun rose.

I've been in Boulder for a week. Starting at around 9,700 feet, entering the Keyhole around 13,000. Many painful leg moments to overcome. Up to the boulder field the trail is relatively normal Colorado; then rocky and hoppy at the boulder field; then a mix of scrambling and climbing after the Keyhole.

Longs is by far the most technical 14er I've climbed in Colorado, and my favorite so far. I was surprised there weren't more people roped up on the Homestretch because there were people slipping and panicking. Helped some folks off ledges and talked to other climbers about conditions and shoes.

The hike down was long and painful, with a headache the whole way, having to stop several times to just sit and breathe. As predicted, it started to rain around 1:30pm, which turned to hail and poured. I learned how permeable my "water resistant" windbreaker is, and quickly became soaked.

When I got to the car and drove back to Boulder, the roads were completely dry within ten minutes.

But yeah, I'd do it again.
