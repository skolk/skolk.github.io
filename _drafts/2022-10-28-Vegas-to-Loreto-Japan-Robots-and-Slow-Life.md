Japan, Vegas, Loreto. Abstract logic, motion path planning, trains, shrines, forest walks, a space mission on the ISS, the state of satellite servicing, how remote lunar space stations can operate without humans, to boat projects, working in the Mexican heat, tracking oil leaks, and separating oil from water while learning about different ablative paint types and how they affect the marine environment.

We'll be down here, off and on, for the next few months, working on Petrichor and working remotely. Life is slower here. The fishermen take naps. There's less rush in almost everything. There are musical groups wandering the streets, sometimes looking for a restaurant to play at, sometimes taking a corner and just enjoying themselves.

Already I'm writing more at cafes. The commons of Loreto continues to invite dogs, musicians, and sailors to bask. Time to go work on the boat again.
