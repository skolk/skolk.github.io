A friend nudged me to go into the place I wanted to go and bother a working man. He turned out to be happy to talk.

John has been roasting, teaching cupping and barista training, and collecting and repairing coffee roasters from around the world for over 20 years. He told us about his work with USAID in East Africa working with growers and suppliers, his efforts in the Caribbean to grow and supply beans, and a project involving the purchase of a cargo ship to ensure timely distribution.

He was involved in every step: taught, supplied, fixed capital tools, and talked to lost sailors in search of coffee, explaining the notes in light and medium roasts and why we should try the Sumatra and the Brazilian, prepared differently. After half an hour he told us he had to get back to roasting because he was flying to Morocco the next day to pick up another roaster. If we came back in the morning, he could provide some beans. We were at his door shortly after sunrise.

He had picked a field, knew it could help people, and wasn't afraid to expand his definition of where he could go or what he could do.

A month later I'm sitting here wondering what I could provide in the same spirit, with similar diligence and focus. What's the gift you give away? How much time do you spend searching versus just getting into it? The problems aren't hard to find. Naval gazing isn't helping anyone. What's the task at hand? You probably already know.
