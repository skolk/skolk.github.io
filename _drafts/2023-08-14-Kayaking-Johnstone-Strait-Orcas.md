I'd spent the previous week at a space conference talking about the future of small satellites, what autonomy means, and the various ways one can use an aerospace degree. Three flights later, landing on Vancouver Island, Kate picked me up and we drove along the inland passage to Telegraph Cove to meet her parents for dinner in the old logging town turned whale-watching destination.

We met our group the following morning: 11 of us to kayak for four days around Johnstone and Blackfish Straits, camping in the western cedars and watching humpbacks rise through the ever-present fog.

We learned to compensate for current in the straits and where to put camp above the rising tides. Bald eagles and kingfishers played around the coves. Meteor showers at night.

Our last day, after three days of watching numerous humpback whales breach, bubble-feed, and surface to sunsets, we were about to paddle back to base and drive home when a resident orca pod that had just returned to the area appeared, playing with a pod of dolphins less than 300 feet from us.

Happy August.
