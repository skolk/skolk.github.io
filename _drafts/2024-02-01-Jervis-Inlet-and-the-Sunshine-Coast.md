Two years after sailing down to Mexico, I didn't expect to be looking at snow, dealing with frost, and debating whether to bring just skis or crampons as well.

This past month: motoring up from Seattle into the Gulf Islands, a week at anchor in Vancouver, then working our way up the Strait of Georgia, through Jervis Inlet, a rapid, and into a glacially carved inlet with fog, mist, rain, and snow-capped peaks. I'm more excited than ever to have explored the Sunshine Coast and the northern half of the Salish Sea. The great big yonder keeps getting smaller; the people friendlier; the run-ins with similar souls more frequent.

Time to take what we've learned and put it into service.
