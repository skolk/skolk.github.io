Kate and I spent the last week in Mexico City in the Condesa neighborhood, wandering the streets in the mornings and evenings, and working from our shaded apartment during the day, located behind a cafe where a string quartet (or a slow jazz trumpet, or a wandering accordion) played. Vines grew slowly over the stucco and cinderblock houses along the avenues as light rain fed the city's reclaimed greenery. At least one cafe every block, accompanied by two street taco stands, a local dog and possibly a cat, and a police officer directing traffic or talking gossip with a business owner.

One can walk here: the sidewalks and plazas, the parks and the streets closed on Saturday. It's a sprawl, with mountains and hills eventually pushing back on the urban. The more expensive areas tend to have more shade and older tree growth, a luxury the box houses lack.

On the weekend we drove up to the mountains with a guide for transport and logistics. A 5 am departure made for quick travel. We started our trek at 13,000 feet and scrambled the rim of the crater up to 15,000. I knew it was going to be a headache of a descent.

We could have cooked in the rental, but there were too many restaurants to explore. The pastries, the sushi, the oysters, the dumplings. Just a little more spice in almost everything, and usually cream or milk as well.

Good conversations on local civics, contribution, feminism, balance of power, yoga, cultural appropriation, and art.

Good breathing to you.
