**Log from November 22:**

After a long, slow, frustrating descent in light winds down to Los Gatos, where Kate and I argued about sail plan, how to deal with swell in light winds, and about planning for the future versus dealing with the very near problem here and now, we made it into the anchorage with cut lazy jacks and tired egos.

The first night was completely empty. We had the whole cove to ourselves. Osprey and herons caught fish. Western gulls played in the spray. We'd stopped here the year before, but only for a morning. Now we were here for at least two days, with a calm moving in.

I apologized for my lack of presence from the day before. It's easier to focus on each other when there isn't a large crew around.

The following day we went ashore and hiked the rocky outcrops. A sea kayaking group had their eco camp on the far side of the cove. Walking on the coast provided different conversations than those you have facing the horizon from the sea.

The calm gave us time to work on rigging, hoist jack lines, fix the burgee line that had come down last season, and work on wood projects and fiberglass repairs with Marine-Tex. These things are never ending.

That evening Silver Lining dropped anchor. We'd met them on the Ha-Ha the year before. They gave us advice on the engine, the weather, and the seas. At the morning we left at dawn; they sent pictures a few days later.
