Great Northern California, accidental loop. SF to Weaverville: found out there are bald-faced hornets in California that can squirt venom in your eyes. Wary campers told us not to walk toward their nest, so we slept with the windows shut.

Drove up Canyon Creek in the morning for a 12-mile hike, then about 10 minutes up realized that wasn't fog, it was smoke, and turned around for the ranger station. Found some locals with a deer in their truck. They told us about a 500-acre fire jumping creeks and roads, so we drove to Eureka.

Went to Redwoods National Park (new favorite). Hiked 12 miles, explored an amazing fern canyon, went to a hot tub cafe in Arcata, slept in the Avenue of the Giants. Drove to Healdsburg, hiked and pack-rafted the Russian River, 3 miles on tame water. Then went sailing in Sausalito for an hour to watch the sunset, and got a PFD back.

Almost none of this was planned. We did have the packrafts.
