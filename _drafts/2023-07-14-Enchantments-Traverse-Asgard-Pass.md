In one last effort to find snow before it turned to ice, Kate and I rushed up to Lake Colchuck and over Asgard Pass to complete the Enchantments traverse. It'd been on our list for a while, and it delivered.

At 4 am we woke up and drove to an already-packed parking lot (we got the last spot out of about 50), made coffee, and joined 30 other hikers in a van pool to the Stewart Lake Trailhead 30 minutes away. Once there, around 5:30 am, it was a madhouse of people signing in for their permits. We pushed through some slower groups and made it to the lake at around 3 mph, then up the pass before 9 am as the sun started to take our shade.

At the pass we could see Glacier Peak, Mount Cashmere (which we'd climbed in June), and other peaks to the north. It felt like the hard part was over, but the mountain goats and friendly alpine lakes were misleading: we still had 14 miles of descent to go.

We sauntered through the valley, which reminded me of the High Sierra, watching parties swim glacial lakes and many a goat family munch on grass. I pushed to keep moving, which was probably right, as we later read that many parties didn't finish until 8 or 9 pm. We got out at 5:30, drove home to a shower and Thai food.

Our knees still hate us.
