A weekend trip turned into an overnight push when weather moved in. We hiked 2,000 feet Friday, set camp, then left at 4 a.m. Saturday for the summit, teaching Tristan to self-arrest and rope up on the way. Amazing to get this much sun. There was a thick cloud layer at 7,000 feet that turned a few parties back, but we pushed through and got wind-blasted into a clear sky view with no other mountains visible, just Mount Baker above the clouds. Good 10,000-foot peak, probably my favorite training summit for anyone ready to step onto a glacier.
