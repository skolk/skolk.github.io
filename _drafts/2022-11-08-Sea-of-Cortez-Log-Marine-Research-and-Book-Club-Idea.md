A friend and I met at a gathering in Seattle for a boat project and hit it off talking about the PNW, his boat, and marine conservation. Turns out he has an old Canadian Coast Guard vessel that sleeps about 10, and we started talking about how amateur and professional science pipelines could be expanded by getting people who care, and who actually do things, to help on working passages down in Baja.

So here I am, not far from where Steinbeck and Ed Ricketts collected specimens, listening to the Sea of Cortez with the goal of starting something that begins as a book club and turns into a movement. How do we, those who spend so much time on the water, give back? We start by inviting others out: bring explorers, provide paths, show how you can help. There are reefs to protect and seagrass to tend.

So I invite you to read, or listen to, "The Log from the Sea of Cortez" and explore what your own mini expedition might be. What can you learn about the wildlife around you?

[iNaturalist](https://www.inaturalist.org/) is an excellent tool for starting to understand the nature around you.
