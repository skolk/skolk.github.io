Move with what you have. Make plays with the water that's there.

The tides rolled us toward the Pacific again. A tugboat emerged out of the gray shroud and submerged back as it sank behind us. We waded toward the Broughtons in complex currents, bald eagles chipping far off in the distance. The day before had given us a gift of passage, and we didn't squander it. Cell towers never made it into this part of the coast. All we had was our crew, the food we used to share creativity, and the occasional book or small project to stay present. Being on the boat was simple: make food, keep going, tend to those around you.

We made Port Hardy by sunset. Crew turnover set for the next day, we were fulfilled, and I was pleasantly exhausted.
