Getting out on the water with new sailors: six different boats in four weeks between Lake Union and Shilshole. Seattle continues to be a great sailing community.

Last night I was introduced to the Catalina 36 Mata Hari, and we got out for the last in a series I should probably pay more attention to. We had a crew eager to learn and step up, so we had beginners jumping ropes, grinding, and learning about lift, surface area, and hull speed. It's so much fun to introduce people into a whole new world of math and physics.

The after party kept bringing in more wild and wonderful people. The conversation kept circling back to how to get more people on the water. So far, sailing with boats like Mata Hari, which take on more crew than they need and give them real jobs with an intro and feedback, is a great first step. Out on the water last night there were several boats with all-women crews and active coaches volunteering their time to make sure it wasn't a toxic shouting mess.

Looking forward to the upcoming events and races and projects, and to more ways to be on and with the water.
