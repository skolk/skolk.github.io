After 48 hours working on the boat, Kate and I went on a road trip up the coast to perform our long-deferred ceremony of road-trip therapy. We left Loreto before sunrise, orange and purple mountains scattered with 10-meter-tall cactus, debating the origin of equality, species adaptation to the desert, and the wind patterns that flow down the Sea of Cortez.

At our destination, paddle boards inflated among the Sprinters and RVs migrating south for the winter, we made our way to a small island to watch kingfishers, osprey, and brown pelicans. We had lunch on our boards, and on the way back spotted a cruiser we'd met in the marina the previous season. They invited us aboard for coffee, and introduced us to their friends who had driven down from Oregon.

Meeting cruisers on their vessels is always good: you see how they host, what they have underway, and get to ask about their processes. Dragon Star is a wealth of knowledge, and gave us recommendations for the Sea of Cortez and for sailing around Vancouver.

Kate and I paddled back to shore, packed the boards into the tiny rental, and made our way to town. Walked through the oasis surrounded by desert, saw the mission and the lighthouse, then drove back planning fiberglass projects.

Off as early as tomorrow for a week north to San Carlos, then La Paz. Loreto has been a wonderful month, meeting locals and slow travelers alike.
