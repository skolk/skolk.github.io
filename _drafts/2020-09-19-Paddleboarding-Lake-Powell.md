The vehicles we move through life in change how and what we see. I learned to kayak before I could canoe, and have been learning the slow, deliberate strokes it takes to steer and maneuver a paddleboard. Paddling is a slow and deliberate effort, one that forces you to look around and acknowledge the wind, waves, current, and obstacles.

This past week at Lake Powell, Kate and I took out hard boards among more than a few powerboats and choppy water. I made an effort to stand through the crossings, white-knuckling it at times, and we were rewarded with sandstone canyons, Bighorn Sheep, and Great Blue Herons.

Paddleboarding is a good balance for the two of us. It offers a different perspective for wildlife watching while letting me push myself in choppy water and Kate sit and let the landscape come to her.

It was a good day, ending with wine and pesto away from the shore as the desert walls caught the sunset.
