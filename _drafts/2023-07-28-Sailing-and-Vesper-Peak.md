Kate caught the fact that I'd been sailing five days last week: Ballard Cup on one boat, Duck Dodge on another (happy place), Elliot Bay with a crew that won R2AK this year, and then Take Your Time Fridays out of Shilshole on Petrichor. We almost drifted into a neighboring boat in a wind hole and motored back rather than deal with it. We flew kites on every race, asymmetric or our symmetric spinnaker, and didn't have any major foul-ups.

The pace of sailing on different boats, in different conditions, with different crews has been an amazing education: racing, team communication, all of which we get to bring back to our own boat and crew.

I haven't said publicly yet, but we're planning to race to Alaska next year. Getting to understand what multi-day racing in rough conditions does to people, what breaks and what holds, has been the point of all this.

To counter the sailing, Kate and I summited Vesper Peak on Saturday. A 4,500-foot, 8-hour day hike. It was probably six months ago we were only doing 1,000-to-2,000-foot peaks, and the progression with hiking PT and lifting has been worth it. Definitely thinking about what's next for this fall and winter.

After all the sailing and peak-bagging, the rest of the weekend felt easy: birthday parties, farmers markets, brunch, boat repairs, listening to books on dopamine and anxiety. Just lift stuff. Happy snapshot of life.
