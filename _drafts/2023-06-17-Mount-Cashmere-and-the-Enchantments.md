Kate and I set out to the east side of the Cascades for fear of weather this week, expecting day hikes. The ranger told us there were permits available, so we snagged one to head back up Mount Cashmere late in the day, with plans for at least one overnight.

Going lightish, with two 30-liter packs and our spring snow gear (crampons, ice axe, rope), we hiked up to a lake 4 miles in and 3,000 feet up, set a tent, and passed out at 9 pm, probably sleeping 11 hours to make up for the week.

The next day we wound our way to the ridge for fantastic views of the Enchantments, Lake Colchuck and Asgard Pass, along with two new birds and several new wildflowers (all on iNaturalist). I went a little off route on the summit ascent, and we did a few class 5 moves that could have been avoided and crossed some steep ice that could have been safer. So now we have a safe word for when I'm pushing past the agreed limits.

Anyway. We met some climbers from the Washington Alpine Club, who told us about new areas to explore, and we had a fruit-rollup eating race at the summit. Then we roamed down, packed up, and headed back to Leavenworth to eat German things and drive to Seattle to unload and hang wet gear. We'll be back at it next weekend.
