Day Five.

We'd gone outside Fitz Hugh Sound and around Calvert Island to Hakai Passage, headed into the Beard Islands, a protected bird sanctuary that would lead us to Bella Bella, our required checkpoint.

The wind shifted on and off, but we pedaled with the larger foresails and then would get another gust and shift to kites. By the time we reached the island-filled passage leading back to Bella Bella, we were happily cruising at 5 knots downwind.

We saw shell middens along the coast as we gybed down the channel, and slowly received cell signal to learn we'd gained 10 nautical miles on Roscoe, with Rock the Boat behind them and Juvenile Delinquents having made ground.

Unfortunately, we later learned that Roscoe Pickle Train had an accident and needed to stop in Bella Bella for half a day to assess and recover.

The media team race boat motored alongside us as we passed the halfway mark, and we continued back out toward the Pacific. Weather models updated, and we decided to head back out to the outside passage and take the wind on offer over the protected inside passages.
