My second day in Tokyo: woke up at 5am and started walking. I called Kate and talked through the housing differences, the sidewalks, the lack of car horns. Children walking to school unaccompanied in their uniforms. I walked in my sandals in light rain and fog through small houses packed among cars on sidewalks.

Walked through parks where the public restrooms were clean with heated seats. Walked to the royal palace, closed but filled with birds in the moat. Walked to three shrines, watching tourists and eating vendor food. Walked over train tracks and along riversides, public art on the flood barriers showing the history of shipping and trade. Four parks, each with some kind of shrine, Japanese maples, bamboo, and stone figures.

There are people everywhere, many young, some old, more of a mix than I see in the US. I watched teachers shuffling school children, making them laugh at themselves.

It was slow at times, and often awkward being so conspicuous, but everyone was helpful and polite. Ended my walk with a run, found that my sandals were not out of place, and had a conversation about different methods of making and where they need improvement.

Thanks Tokyo for being so walkable.
