Five of us set out from La Paz to Espiritu Santo this past week. A return to the island where I first chartered a boat under my own name five years ago. A lot has changed since then, both in La Paz and for Kate and me as sailors. The island has mostly stayed the same.

We'd set sail on a Morgan 41 ketch out of Marina Cortez with four friends on one of the most memorable trips I'd had. We learned to spearfish, make duck tacos, coordinate anchoring with other cruisers, and explore cactus-laden hills. It was new. It was whelming in a way I usually describe as being "in the flow."

Half a decade later, Kate and I have sailed four seas more, both high and low latitudes, on expedition boats and racers, bought our boat, and sailed back to this same barren island. We found new tracks to follow. The worries we had under a charter were quite different from those we have for our own boat. The crew dynamics just as fascinating. The conversations and focus of the day, still as wandering.

Good to get in the water and see ourselves in an old place as new people.
