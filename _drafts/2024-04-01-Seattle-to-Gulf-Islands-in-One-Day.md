Heavy-weather pictures are hard to get right. Here we are crossing the Strait of Juan de Fuca after a 4 am start, 20 knots on the beam, 3-plus feet of swell. The current helped us out of Admiralty Inlet, across the strait, and into the San Juans, hitting 10 knots at times under sail.

I hadn't planned to cross into Canada without knowing whether the crew and weather would cooperate, but making Friday Harbor by noon inspired us to call the marina and ask about the Pender Island border crossing. Turns out it's 24/7. After check-in at Poets Cove, we rounded to Pender, dropped anchor, popped the dinghy, and went ashore for land food. Seattle to the Gulf Islands in one day, with sunlight to spare.

Bring good company and a calm mind. The rest follows.
