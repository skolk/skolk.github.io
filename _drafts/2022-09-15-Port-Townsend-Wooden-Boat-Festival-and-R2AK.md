Kate and I went to our first Port Townsend Wooden Boat Festival, where I volunteered on the docks and Kate found all the classes on rigging, sail repair, and women in the boat trades.

Kate found a talk on synthetic rigging by local riggers, covering stretch and creep. We saw a talk by a rower who had rowed from Mongolia through Russia to the Arctic Circle, building a sleep-aboard rowboat along the way. We attended a panel of women in the boat trades, hearing from sailmakers, boatwrights, captains, and riggers about the work to expand diversity in the industry.

We met a sailor from R2AK Team Joker, who sailed to Ketchikan in six days, and talked about provisioning and how to think about sailing at night and in the wind. We got to meet the R2AK race director, lots of rowers and sailors, and learned that many of the racers have their whole lives organized around sailing.

We ran into a friend from Seattle's sailing community, learned what she's working on for education and inclusion in marine communities, and then met a paddler who has SUP'd to Alaska and is planning to paddle around the Arctic.

Then we may have seriously discussed buying a boat for the race next year. Welp.
