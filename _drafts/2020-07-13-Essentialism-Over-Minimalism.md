Most days, this is it.

The issue with minimalism, or maybe more specifically one-bagging, is that my goals require constant context shifts. It doesn't make sense to always carry my ice axes, but when I need them, they're necessary. Essentialism asks what is essential, which forces the question of why you're bringing this or that, rather than just always taking less.

This is generally what I end up with for a weeklong work-and-hike trip, maybe trekking poles for light snow and most 4th-class peaks. I could see leaving the laptop behind depending on the work. I haven't found an upper limit for how long this clothing set can last, washing in sinks and hanging on a cord I carry.
