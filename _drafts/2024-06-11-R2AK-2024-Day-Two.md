Our headstay sprung and our race was in peril. We pedaled along Galiano Island, calling riggers, racers, and any fan in the area, gathering information about our situation and making a plan for repair.

After turning downwind, Brian attached a spinnaker halyard to the bow deck plate, and after assessing, decided another wouldn't hurt. Our rod rigging had snapped up top by the masthead. We lowered the foresail with both stay and foil attached, wrapping the metal and plastic combination around the boat. Once we put down the main, we had two people pedaling against the current toward an island.

Nathalie took the lead on calling riggers, Brian on masthead measurements; Winston and I hoisted Brian up the mast, and we all took measurements of the forestay as it lay mangled around the deck.

All this took place as we pedaled, slowly gaining confidence in our rig with its two-halyard forestay. We finally agreed to put up the hank-on drifter and a three-reefed main. Pedaling in flat water, we could make about 1.8 to 2 knots at the expense of crew, switching out every 20 to 40 minutes depending on the stretch.

Things started to look plausible. We had a boat, with a mast, going toward a marina we'd coordinated with a friend we'd met at an R2AK event the year before. The riggers had assembled the forestay and sent it up from Sidney to Nanaimo. We had a jury-rigged boat moving in the correct direction, and we learned that the previous year's winning team had heard where we were headed and was on its way to assist.

We docked under sail and pedal, right next to a large tower. Unknown to us, planned or not, it was the rigging tower. In under 30 minutes we had the new rig in hand from our delivery crew, and we were up the tower pulling the old hardware and putting in the new.

It took three hours in Nanaimo to replace, tension, and reassemble everything ourselves and get back out on the water, shower and meal included by a gracious local couple, logistics and moorage by friends ashore. We were back out on the water for the night.
