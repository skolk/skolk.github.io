A few people have asked for the calendar template I use to plan the year. I used to do it in Photoshop, then Figma, and lately I've been doing it less and less on a computer and more on a piece of paper.

I take a coffee mug or saucer, put it down, trace a circle. I make four marks to divide the seasons, and start in the middle with something resembling my values or aspirations in the broadest sense: who do I want to be, what would that person do, and what would that result in. These tend to fracture a bit, because working toward a horizon needs small steps along the way to test the theories you're playing with. I've also found that the "one big goal" fulfillable with money usually wasn't as useful as a portfolio of experiments.

This ends up with winter, spring, summer, and fall each having three or four primary foci, which I list on the circle like dates on a calendar, along with some events and trips I have planned. That becomes the circle of intent. I look at it most days, folded in half in my journal. I remake it roughly every quarter and note what stayed consistent. It's less a hard document and more a practice of introspection.

The commitments come if you put work into them. Friends help, but ultimately it's about putting effort in. Breath well.
