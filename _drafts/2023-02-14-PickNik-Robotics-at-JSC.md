About a decade into working in automation and robotics, despite having studied none of it at university. I found my way into electromechanical engineering and software development via patient friends who are generous teachers, and who also saw that I should be working in interpersonal systems, helping makers find who needs what they build.

This week we kicked off a Phase 3 SBIR contract with the ER-4 robotics group at JSC on the future of autonomous systems for low-Earth orbit and cislunar. The team toured the facilities, inspecting lunar habitat prototypes, robotics development projects (Robonaut, Valkyrie, Argos), and walked the ISS control room floor during a routine signal-down period to see how robotics systems are controlled from the ground.

We demoed to the NASA branch chief of software systems, the NASA technology director of robotics, and several other directors of flight programs, showing how our systems can accelerate their directives and how to expand the space robotics community.
