The fog hides the horizon and reminds you that the path is made of moments of presence. Not on the horizon. Here. Now.

We continue to head south, jumping island to island with otters, murres, gulls, and whales around us. Crossing Cape Caution the other day, we came within two boat lengths of a surfacing gray whale in the fog. No hump, callused skin: it took a moment before we recognized it. The other features of the lower passage are starting to emerge. Ospreys, Steller's jays, logs. There were almost no logs in Alaska, but south of Prince Rupert they hug the current lines and mingle around anchorages.

We trade off between shore anchorages where we find a beach to walk and explore the forest, and coves where we paddle and observe from the sea. Both have their merits, as long as I get enough movement in during the day to keep a caffeine-wired mind still.

Days are planned around tides and currents, moving Petrichor down the coast with small projects in between. Tea for cold days, and some for warm as well.

Deep breaths.
