Our second coastal trip outside the Gate. The morning tugs were making wakes hauling container ships through the Oakland Estuary as we motored into SF Bay before dawn. Wind was non-existent coming out the Gate, so we sipped tea rounding Point Bonita and crossing the Potato Patch. Fog was setting in, with early morning fishing boats scattered across the coastal waters.

We passed floating flocks of murres, cormorants, pelicans, and gulls, with the occasional Western Grebe and Common Tern overhead. By the time we passed Bolinas the fog had turned to rain, so we put on foul weather gear. Newly named Petrichor and the dinghy Sulfi made way through the bay without sight of land.

When we reached the white cliffs of Point Reyes, we dropped anchor by the lifeboat station, spotted elephant seals ashore, and had an anchorage entirely to ourselves.

We went ashore briefly with the Portland Pudgy, poking around the rocky shore, then came back aboard. The wind picked up overnight and blew through till morning, so we put up sail for the return to the bay.
