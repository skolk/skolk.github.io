**San Evaristo to La Paz, November 25-27:**

We woke at dawn, raised the main, and flapped in irons before pulling the anchor, a lee shore just south and a rocky point to clear. As soon as we were out into the strait the wind picked up. We were hauling 7 knots and surfing 9 to 11. Earlier in the week we'd been happy to make 3. Those lowly ambitions were gone.

After 25 nautical miles we agreed to aim for La Paz. We'd gotten our sailing in for the week, and fixing the motor on a Friday is easier than a weekend. We dropped anchor 6 nautical miles from town, just on the far side of the marina, and tried to get friends to dinghy over. The marina was under a wind warning and wasn't letting anyone leave.

Pasta for the night, bed early.

Morning: Kate dropped me on a beach, I taxied to town, and put out the problem on the La Paz cruiser net. Got sent on a chase across marine stores and tractor supply shops, eventually finding a mechanic who drove me to a pump-rebuild shop. They rebuilt our freshwater impeller for $60 USD in under four hours. I taxied back to the beach, picked up Kate, and we went to town for dinner with a family friend who had just motored down from Los Angeles.

Back at the boat after sunset, motor running, sleep happy.
