This past weekend Kate and I competed in our first Swiftsure race from Victoria, Canada, down the Strait of Juan de Fuca to Clallam Bay and back. Racing on Captain Jeff's boat Mata Hari with crew we'd all met before, we ferried up to the capital of British Columbia and prepared the day before.

With a spinnaker start and an upwind first leg, we swapped between our 1, 2, and 3 within the first hour. Kate managed the pit and I was on the foredeck with another crew member, getting drenched in 10-to-15 knots and 4-foot swell for the first two hours until we came around the island's protection and through Race Rock into the open strait, where the wind and swell picked up and half the crew went down with seasickness.

Bashing into the waves, we overtook two sloops in our division with a 3 and a reefed main, clocking 6-to-8 knots upwind. We rounded the halfway mark around 5:30 pm after six hours of racing, with half the crew spending quality time at the sink.

On the way back, a fellow racer called to say they'd been demasted. We made way to search and assist, only to be beaten to them by other racers and the coast guard. We crossed the line around midnight, and after race time adjustments, found out we'd made 3rd in our class.

We spent the night on the boat drying out, then two days sailing back to Seattle.
