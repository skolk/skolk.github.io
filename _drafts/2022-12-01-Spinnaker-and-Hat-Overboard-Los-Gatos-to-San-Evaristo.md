**November 23: Los Gatos to San Evaristo:**

We left Las Gatos knowing it might be a light day, but we needed to make headway and decided to fly the spinnaker. I've been running foredeck in Seattle, but I wouldn't say I've mastered it. Flying is different from dropping it. Nothing like teaching someone else how to do something to find your own gaps.

The following day was a wind warning. We didn't want to be caught sailing through a channel in high winds, so we took the light-wind day. Flying the kite isn't the most relaxing sailing, but we held her tight, which made for less headway but easier trimming. We avoided jibing for four hours until we had more wind than we wanted, and then we had to jibe. Chaos ensued, as expected, with the two of us managing halyards. The spinnaker got some water.

After we thought we were done, my hat blew off. We performed an impromptu man-overboard drill and retrieved it, only after three tries. No motor to rely on "just in case." I was honestly amazed the hat was still floating after a 15-knot blow in the choppy strait.

Back under jib, we sailed into San Evaristo and powered up the dinghy, which as of the night before had one full working oar. A Cactus Wren chirped around us on shore as the birds played with fish skeletons.

Back at the boat, we made friends with a sailor who'd been coming down for 20 years. He rowed over and we talked for 30 minutes about the motor before we parted and made dinner.

Another day of learning and breaking things.
