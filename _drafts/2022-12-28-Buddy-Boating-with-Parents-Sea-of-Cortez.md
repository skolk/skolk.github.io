This past week Kate and I buddy-boated with Motor Yacht Stay or Leave, Captain Keith and my parents. While they motored at 10 to 15 knots, we would leave at dawn and arrive at the anchorage around the same time with a very different experience getting there. While our motor works again, we're still sailing anything we can get more than 4 knots out of. We ended up diving at Candelabra off Isla Espiritu Santo, stopping at Isla San Francisco to hike the peak, and crashing through the channel on Christmas.

The way back, though. Kate needed 60 nautical miles as captain on a passage, and we were planning to take the 20s and 30s around Espiritu Santo. We set out in the channel with a double reef, flying 5 to 6 knots in low chop, which picked up constant spray. We reefed the genoa and dropped the main completely. Two-hour watches in rocking swell, couldn't spend much time below. A few broaches where we rounded up, but nothing close to a knockdown. All of this happening while I was listening to Moby Dick: a surreal pairing.

We decided after an hour in the 30s to take the inside of the island rather than going around, hoping to lose some swell by cutting off the fetch behind the island. After 10 hours of sailing we came into Bahia Falsa. Set anchor, made for bed, and left the cleaning for morning.
