A weeklong trip back to the Bay put me in touch with many a friend and a few new faces. I went in to see how time had played with us all.

My friends are still making, building, gathering, and exploring. They're also producing more offspring, though there are new ideas I haven't yet encountered.

In the PNW, Kate and I have been focused on sailing and mountaineering. The minds we meet aren't that different from the Bay.

I keep seeing hard problems being worked on for their own sake, and also people landing their fixation where a solvable sub-problem lives rather than on the bigger thing. It's good to solve a part, if you can solve anything at all, but what was the original intent, and how do you keep that on the horizon?

How big a resource base do you need to make the change you want to see? Do you start by gathering people or capital? How do you measure trust in those scenarios?

Where does stability meet change, and can you afford it in a place with growing disparity? If satisfaction is wants over needs, which is easier to reduce?

Good to see everyone down there. Until our paths cross again.
