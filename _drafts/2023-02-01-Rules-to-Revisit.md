Pilots, skippers, climbers, backcountry folks: what were your rules when you started, and what are they now?

When beginning, it's easy to say "don't play with fire, don't touch the knives." Those are shorthand for: "it's complicated, and understanding the nuance of these tools or conditions takes experience." So early on, rules of thumb help. And yet as you progress, see the nuance, and find the limits of the generalizations, you end up going out in more extreme conditions, using different aspects of an environment, and writing new rules.

So what are some rules worth revisiting? What guides have you found needed updating?
