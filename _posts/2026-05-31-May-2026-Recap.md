The travel-heavy run kept going — Boston and the Harvard museum, free diving the Bahamas with Green Coco aboard *SV Nesi*, a family wedding in Toronto, and out to the Inner Hebrides for an islandness gathering on the Isle of Ulva. In between it all, Seattle and sunset sails on *Petrichor*. The month also closed the long GPS-hardware arc on the Astraeus tracker and landed on the end-of-May revenue checkpoint.

## Travel

- **Boston / Cambridge** — the Harvard Museum of Natural History got me reading about the explorers and idea-people: Robert Peary to the North Pole, George Peabody seeding the anthropology conversations and Darwin's contributions. A reminder that exploration is something you get to be part of. Stayed at Thomas' place in Cambridge — "I can build apps that build apps; hardware might be more valuable than software now."
- **Bahamas** — a week off Exuma with [Green Coco Expeditions](https://greencoco.org/) on the 60-foot catamaran *Nesi*; through Hog Cut to Stone Cay, Water Cay, and Flamingo Key. Blue holes, loggerhead turtles, sharks, jackfish, dives past 10m. Best charter I've been on — Jana, Sabrina, and Kristian made it generous and easy, always lowering the barrier to get people in the water. Want to bring that to our own trips.
- **Toronto** — family time with Kate, Sue, and Uncle Mike around a wedding.
- **Scotland (Glasgow → Oban → Ulva)** — Hannah Close's Islandness gathering on Ulva, part-residency, part-shoreside philosophy salon. Talked with Andy and Yvette about the community land buyout, deer and venison, salmon farms, and the hard governance work that comes after you buy an island. Exactly the coastal-community work I want to be building tools for.
- **Seattle** — home base between legs; *Petrichor* out when the weather allowed.
