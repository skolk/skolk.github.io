I don't like the idea that I consume, but don't put effort into what I consume. Food comes from somewhere, yet not knowing my farmers, or event the people at my grocery store, I'm fed and have little to no tangible connection to what I consume.

I want to change, that yet not change my living situation. With the understanding that there are community gardens, and spaces where I could grow food (or offset the cost, by paying other in a more direct way- without a middle man).

The idea so far has sprouted from the inverse idea of my pickle jar. Pickling has been an activity my partner and I have involved ourselves in for the last year or two. It requires water, salt, a leafy green, or vegetable, and a jar. That's it. It's one of the first ways of preserving food, and one of the safest ways of keeping veggies from rotting (as long as they're sub merged, and not exposed to air).

The same tool that I use to pickle (a jar). I would like to experiment in growing food. A tiny ecosystem of growth and storage (minus the dirt). What if you could have a line of jars on your wall, some growing, other storing, all viable options for consumption.
