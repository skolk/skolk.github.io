**Mostly Seattle.** Boat out. Vesper Peak as the Snoqualmie alternative (it had already snowed).

## Projects

**Astraeus.** Big month. Built out TidalSense, the persistent maritime domain awareness platform. Think Planet Labs for the sea. $10K initial assessment plus $50 per acre per month. $25K Taylor Shellfish proposal submitted. Drayton Harbor 5-acre pilot. Follow-up letter to Tracie at WA DOH on PSP and Vibrio integration. ZOC Platform deck for Ocean Week investor meetings.

**Corumat.** Most pressing paying client. $500K capital and pilots target by mid-December. Mike's feedback landed hard and right: I need you to approach these companies as a pilot salesman, not as a packaging vendor. Letters of support. SnoCap and E8 outreach.

**Quantum Alliance.** Oct 15 Harvard Club meeting with Marc Carena (Mars / FoodDrinkEurope). Marc's message landed hard: STOP GIVING ME THEORETICAL AREAS. We are not lacking consultants. Pivot to project-based service provider. UAE sponsorship commitment via Ujjwal.

**REAP.** Oct 8 CAJF RII Round 2 with Jonathan DeLong and Nicole Greenspan. NOI due Nov 5. Pre-App Dec 3. Full App Jan 16.

**SASD and consulting.** Jonathan DeLong 3-4 month proposal-writing engagement scoping. Big Green Loop Manufacturing with Mike and Corey Matt. Paola from FoodDrinkEurope follow-ups (sargassum prediction, coral die-off data products).

**For Blue (Australia).** Oct 21. Economic-complexity-aligned natural-resources mapping group.

**The ship.** Oct 30-31 aboard the [Statsraad Lehmkuhl](https://www.lehmkuhl.no/en/). A 300 foot barque. ~90 souls. 30 crew. 60 trainees. Maritime delegation sailing Seattle to SF. The second day at sea produced 3 to 4 meter swell and put a third of the crew seasick.

**Finds.** Mitch Ratcliffe dinner Oct 2 (continues to inspire questions). Rex Raimond on captive risk pools. Jonathan DeLong on the RII. Marc Carena: gateway to Amanda David (head of procurement for all of Mars) and Greg Hopkins. Ujjwal co-pitching Mars. Tracie at WA DOH. Mike Waggoner. Saturday and Sunday dinner with friends in Seattle. Paola de Almeida. Nanna, the 25-year-old sailor on the Statsraad, archetype of the competent young European who knows she can climb a rig and set a course. Josh onboard, closer to a politician. Mythos in NYC, being followed up with.

**Writing.** Bitter Tea for Bitter Sailors project notes (values: connection, reflection, comradery). The long ship-board essay (Oct 31) referencing Neal Stephenson's *Diamond Age* and James P. Carse's *Finite and Infinite Games*. Mars TidalSense investor deck. DOH outreach letter to Tracie. Mythos follow-up draft.

**Reading.** *Maoyuu Maou Yuusha* (demon economics anime).

**Lines.** You get what you can give. It is extremely transactional. It puts you in a set of games. The sailors take all of this for granted: they know they're valuable, what they do is difficult, and that they can and will get on another ship. "Always be providing value" is a dangerous short game to always play, but also a beneficial one. Make lots of small projects and put them out there. To be of service: start where you are, do what you can. The Marc Carena bell that won't stop ringing: don't be seen as another consultant.

**Looking ahead.** Follow-ups with the NYC Climate Week contacts. SF, Mexico, Seattle, Costa Rica, Panama travel arc. Bird sanctuaries in Central America. Coastal mariculture community work. CAJF RII Notice of Intent Nov 5. Corumat money in the door by mid-December. Bitter Tea, "Paths and Consequences" game. A talk on "a path into sailing." On being of service.
