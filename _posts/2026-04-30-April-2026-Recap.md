A transition month in Seattle: a false spring, then a harsh push back into winter. I took *Petrichor* out as much as I could for sunset sails, overnights in Port Ludlow and Port Orchard with the Sloop Tavern Yacht Club, and spent the rest on projects around the water, getting ready for summer travels and for Kate back in the northern hemisphere.

## Projects

- **[RYA](https://www.rya.org.uk/) Yachtmaster training**: many hours over charts, memorizing the light patterns for ships in the night.
- **RYA Radio course**: the requirements of radio and GNSS communication at sea.
- **Advanced First Aid for sailors**: re-certified as part of the prep, since it had been over two years.
- **Modeling**: learning ways to map, project, and emulate the world.
- **Biological digital twin**: a project to predict the nominal and off-nominal state of biological objects.
- **Ecosystem mapping (ecological-economic)**: progress on a framework to map coastlines and their environmental conditions.
- **Community sense-making**: the work above is all in service of groups finding ways to come together and make decisions.

## Finds

- Nora Bateson and [Warm Data](https://www.warmdata.life/): sat in on a Warm Data event.

## Writing

- **The Generative Spark**: what inspires action; identity as fuel vs. cage, and what "leading by inviting" looks like. Pair the big vision with the intermediate steps and the community around it.
- **Reconciling abundance and scarcity**: acceptance vs. agency; techno-utopian tool-crafters against land-based people who work *with* firm limits, "being with rather than taking from."
- **Math-induced sailing doubt**: RYA theory as abstraction vs. the confidence you only build on the water. "Here's your permission. Go."
- **Capabilities, first aid and risk for your trips**: how a capabilities question becomes a risk-management one; de-risk by practice, not armchair what-ifs.
- **First time out sailing**: a practical onboarding guide for taking friends out on *Petrichor*.
- **Threads in progress**: the democratic facilitation piece; Mitch Ratcliffe's "feet to the fire" welding/melding; John Clark Mills on organic scientists as local sense-makers.
