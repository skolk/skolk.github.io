The recent increase of seaweed blight along the Caribbean has forced the carbon markets to devalue the previously reliable CO2-capturing ability of the Florida shelf colonies. The Panasonic - Mondragon Co. is moving its graphite production floating factories, dependent on the kelp harvesting, northward early this year to the Hudson Bay and the North Canadian Aquatic-Green Ecosystem.

This market destabilization and the crowded coastal aquatic cultivation areas (sounding:100m up to 12miles offshore) along the entire North American continent, have provoked new off coast experiments by the Ocean Frontier Foundation in exploring the viability of the Wandering Kelp Forest Project. The floating kelp forests would contain the required infrastructure to generate kelp and other traditionally coastal based life off coast at a depth of 30-10m, so avoiding shipping traffic and weather anomalies. Standardized at 1 hectare per unit, the largest test projects have interlocked over 100 hectares to drift the Pacific Gyre for up to one year at a time with minimal interference to shipping traffic.

One of the main challenges to the Wandering Kelp Forest Project has been it’s structural longevity in maintaining neutral buoyancy at sea for while accruing biomass. The Ocean Frontier team breakthroughs in elastic polymer fiber technologies and active pressure monitoring systems similar to the cargo dirigible—now in operation over Central Asia—that use helium as ballast, have allowed the Foundation to gain the confidence of industry skeptics.

Robotic monitoring and harvesting have created new niche industries of locally engineered defensive biological units to target the mites and lionfish. These problem species can permeate the experimental Wandering Kelp Forests if not monitored and destroy the defensive outer nitrogen ‘fence’ kelp and filter mollusks. The micro and macro plastics still pose a threat to the Forests, which use a combination of biological and robotics defenses to ensure a productive and lively ecosystem of kelp, mollusks, and feeder fish. Recent Forests have recorded accounts of being productive enough bio activity to attract whale sharks and manta rays, species normally associated with coastal populations.

The Wandering Kelp Forests are expected to receive full scale implementation in the next 2-5 years with contracts pending with both Exxon Pacific Energy Company and BP-Atlantic, and increase carbon capture of 70 gigatons by 2060.
