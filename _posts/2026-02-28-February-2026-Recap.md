February opened in Barcelona at the tail of the European trip, then home to Seattle for a stretch, then south to the British Virgin Islands for a charter aboard *SV Whichey*. A month that pivoted from city-walking to sailing.

## Travel

- **Barcelona**: closing out the European loop. The easiest of the European cities to walk — widest streets, most car restrictions, a grid that gives way to walking corridors.
- **Seattle**: in between, catching up on RYA Yachtmaster coursework and meetings.
- **British Virgin Islands**: a week of chartering aboard *SV Whichey*. National park permits, mooring at BoatyBall reservations, the early-morning rush to grab a good ball before 7am. A reminder that even paradise has logistics.

## Projects

- **RYA Yachtmaster prep**: real hours into the syllabus this month. Tides, charts, lights, collision regs. The big folder of material started to feel less foreign.
- **Astraeus**: ongoing conversations on coastal mariculture and ecological-economic mapping.
- **[Future Food Institute](https://futurefoodinstitute.org/)**: early conversations about how Astraeus and FFI might align.

## Finds

- **Lara Stein**: met to talk about her work; rich conversation.
- Conversations with **Alex** on long-running themes (a working session that ran to a hundred-plus pages of notes).
- The BVI charter briefing as a study in operational risk — what the briefing covers and what it omits.

## Writing

- **Alan Moore on art as magic**: working through Moore's argument that art and magic are the same operation, the writer as practitioner of intent. A long thread; still drafting.
- **Cooperation**: a seed.
- **Anti doom-scroll manifesto**: a short piece on attention as a political act.
- **Prayers**: a quiet seed.

## Reading

- *Pluralism vs. Universalism: A Critical Reading List* — assembled a long set of texts to work through this question. Still in progress.
- Continuing into the neoliberalism history that I'd finish in March.
