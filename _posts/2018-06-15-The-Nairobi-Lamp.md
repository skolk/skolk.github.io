## The Nairobi Lamp
<img src="/images/blog_posts/image58.jpg" width="100%">

Made from a tin can, local wood, and molded cement the Nairobi Lamp is an experiment in teaching. I arrived in Kenya with the knowledge that Kenyans are entrepreneurial people, almost everyone has a side hustle,  and that I had access to markets that many craftspersons didn’t. So I set about to make a product that could be replicated from local materials, teach new usable skills, and solve a need I and others have. Using only hand tools, and locally sourced electric parts, I began making the lamp (you can see a few other prototypes on my blog) and after some traction (read: 'can I buy one of those?') I began teaching my neighborhood gate guard  to strip wires, and assemble housings. Thus, the Nairobi Lamp was born. I'll be updating with videos and blog post showing how their made, and the process of scaling in the up coming weeks.

Shipping outside Kenya is possible, but I’ll need a critical mass (about 10) before it makes sense, and will be around 20USD. Send me an email if you’re interested.
