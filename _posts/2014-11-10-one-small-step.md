This past weekend I watched the documentary "[Gasland](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gasland#Negative)." About a Pennsylvania mans questions about the issues with hydraulic fracturing (known as fracking) around the US. He travels as far west as Wyoming on a road trip, interviewing famers and residence who have agreements with natural gas companies to drill on their land, or who live in towns where hydraulic fracturing is pervasive. The most notable image of the documentary is when a farmer  lights his tap water (from an onsite well) on fire, due to the pollutants supposedly due tot he local drilling. Josh Fox, the films director, then shows other health issues, with towns populations, local animals, dying fish, the political and social issues around towns that have endured fracking, and just how many people are affected by Fracking in the US.

Gasland is one of about 4 or five documentaries over the past 12 months that have made me angry, sad, and frustrated. All at the same time. They present a situation that is not clearly 'evil,' in that; society does in a way benefit, but shows the aggressiveness of capitalism and short term thinking of how we interact with the world. Only through the frame works constructed by "On the Banality of Evil' does the situation make any sense, as to how it could have come to be this way.

In a separate tangent of my life. I've been enjoying Alastair Humphreys writings on '[micro adventures](http://www.alastairhumphreys.com/microadventures-3/)' which point out that the idea of 'adventure' is not something that needs to be an epic journey. I can be done... even in a night. A small step. An action with intention.

I believe one of the reasons that people feel frustrated with the world is they don't see a way to influence their reality. It's like hitting water. You give money to the 'Hope & Change' campaign and the same shit happens, 40 years go by and cops are still shooting black kids disproportionately more, the voting rights act seems to be disappearing.

I'm tired of... just being a side liner, but am scared that my efforts, time and, money will be stretched thin if I try to support all the causes that I wish were changes in the world i want to see. I go to salons, to talks, to see more movies, to events that ask for donations, to dinners. Talk is cheap. I want to Act, even if it's small. Even if it's just my pebble. It's MY pebble. It will be unique, and funny colored, and awkward, and great.

So I'm going to put in my effort. Just a small effort. But an effort. I want to see what that small step feels like, on those things, that make me angry, mad, and disheartened. I want to see what happens when I spend one night a week on a project, that much like I plan for bike camping on weeknights, or peak climbing of my every week.

Issue. Small actionable step. Person.

Gasland. Water quality. Sean.

Lets see where this goes.
