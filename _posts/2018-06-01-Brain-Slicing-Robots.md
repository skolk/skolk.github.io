## Whole Mount Stain
This is a whole mount stain statin. We invented a new way to stain and then slice organic tissue, and needed a better way to scale staining multiple samples. The tissue was being inserted into the tubes, then heated and stored at tracked temperatures. I would then drain the stain, and flush the system with water, and then follow up with an additional stain.

## Brain Slicing Robot
I worked on Brain Slicing Robots for 6 years in San Francisco to better understand brains, cancer, and neurons. I worked mostly on liquid handling systems, which turned into mechatronics, PCB design, and design for manufacturing once we made about 20 units with a contract manufacture. It was an amazing experience and I owe much of what I know about engineering and design to working with 3Scan.

<img src="/images/blog_posts/image5.jpg" width="100%">

While we fought for years about our value add, we realized that our original prototyping tool (the arduino) wasn’t normally used in precision bio tooling… it worked, and we didn’t need to reinvent the processor to produce a working product, so we made a functioning shield with a fraction of the time and energy.

<img src="/images/blog_posts/image23.jpg" width="100%">

This is part of the build when we moved to a contract manufacture (my part at least, there were several other aspects to this machine). Working month to take years of prototypes and ideas and convey it to another company was a great process to force everything into a finalized product.

<img src="/images/blog_posts/image19.jpg" width="100%">

I don’t believe this was the 1st iteration, probably the 2nd or 3rd, but it went from a mechanical ‘dead-bug’ (where the pieces all work, but they not packaged well) to a box. Boxes are nice, it means you can close them and not everything breaks. We had to create water flow, filter out large materials, heat water, then test for flow, temperature, control some flow mechanisms, and later test for pH and other factors.
