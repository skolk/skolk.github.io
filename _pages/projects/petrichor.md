# SV Petrichor

*Petrichor* is a 1990 Catalina 36 Mk I: my first owned cruising boat, the platform for two offshore seasons in Mexico and a season up the Inside Passage to Southeast Alaska, and now my floating home at Shilshole Marina in Ballard, Seattle. This page is the project frame for the boat herself: the finding, the fitting out, and the long arc of making a small boat carry a big life.

## Before Petrichor: learning to sail

The path to *Petrichor* ran through smaller boats and a community. I owned a 1969 Coronado 25, *Entropy*, from 2012 to 2014, and the crew and sailing friends came largely through the Washed Up Yacht Club. We also sailed with [Cal Sailing](https://www.cal-sailing.org/) around 2016-2017 to learn dinghy sailing. Wanting to travel, I earned my ASA Basic Keelboat and Coastal Cruiser certifications and chartered in Thailand, Croatia, Greece, and the Seychelles.

## Finding and deciding (August 2020)

We bought *Petrichor* in August 2020, once it was clear the pandemic was not ending any time soon. We were actually looking for a day sailor in the 10 to 15k range. A friend suggested we try a boat sitting on their dock, bigger and more expensive than what we had in mind. We took her out for a trial and realized she would shift our lives and what was possible. So we committed: got her surveyed, got her repainted, and all at once we had a huge commitment.

The Catalina 36 is a roomy, well-supported coastal cruiser: not a purpose-built bluewater boat, but a forgiving and affordable one with a deep owner community and parts you can actually source.

## The first year: learning her

We spent about a year sailing her around San Francisco: out the Gate, down to Half Moon Bay, up to Bodega, and inland to Stockton. The more we sailed, the more we wanted, and that is what pointed us at the Baja Ha-Ha and a season in Mexico.

## Fitting out and progressing

A Catalina 36 is a small boat for the trips she has done. Most of the project has been the slow work of making her capable: ground tackle and anchoring for nights on the hook, downwind sail combinations for the trades, power and water for living aboard, and the mechanical self-reliance that long passages and remote anchorages demand. What changed about the boat before each season tells most of the story of what cruising actually requires.

## The voyages

- **[Baja Ha-Ha 2021, Oakland to Cabo]({% post_url 2021-11-01-baja-mexico-2021 %})**: My first major offshore passage captaining my own boat. Down the California coast and into the rally flotilla south along Baja into warmer water.
- **[A season in the Sea of Cortez, 2021-2022]({% post_url 2022-04-01-mexico-2021-2022-cruise %})**: Months working north into the Cortez and back, capped by double-handing *Petrichor* engineless from Loreto to La Paz, anchoring entirely under sail.
- **[The Inside Passage to Southeast Alaska]({% post_url 2026-05-15-alaska-petrichor %})**: Shilshole to Southeast Alaska under her own keel: tide gates, fjord weather, remote anchorages, and the wildlife density of the route north of Cape Caution. (Trip report in progress.)

## Living aboard

*Petrichor* is home. She sits at Shilshole Marina in Ballard, and day-to-day life happens aboard: work from the boat, sunset sails on Puget Sound, and a steady stream of friends taken out for their [first time on the water]({% post_url 2026-04-11-First-Time-Out-Sailing %}). Living aboard is its own discipline, the same self-reliance the passages teach, applied to every ordinary day.

## Status

Active. Home port Shilshole (Ballard, Seattle). This page is a draft; the finding, the refit log, and the full Alaska write-up are still to come.
