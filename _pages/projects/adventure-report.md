# Adventure Report

I started Adventure Report in 2014: a monthly adventure storytelling session. More than a decade later it is still running, and it is one of the most meaningful community projects I have been part of.

## The idea

Everyone has adventures, but few people have a place to share them meaningfully. Social media gives you likes; it does not give you the experience of sitting with someone over coffee while they tell you about getting lost in Patagonia or learning to surf in their 40s. Adventure Report is a space where the story matters more than the photo.

## The format

- When: the first Saturday morning of every month.
- Where: cafes and venues around San Francisco, later other cities.
- What: five or six people share eight to ten minute stories about recent adventures.
- Rules: stories are about something you actually did, no slides, no preparation. Adventure is defined broadly, from an international expedition to trying a new restaurant.

## Why it works

Consistency (same time every month for over ten years), low barriers (no fees, no required prep, you just need a story), and real face-to-face connection. Regular attendees become friends, plan trips together, and support each other's goals.

## Status

Active. Still meeting in multiple cities, and the format has spawned similar groups. Related thinking: [Social Anchors]({% post_url 2017-06-15-social-anchors-post %}).
