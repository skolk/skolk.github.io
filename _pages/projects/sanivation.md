# Sanivation

In 2012, Andrew, Emily, Chris, Nick, and I worked together on the inception of [Sanivation](https://sanivation.com/): a company using solar ovens to heat and dehydrate human waste to render it inert. We were accepted into Start-Up Chile in Santiago for six months. The company was founded in Chile and now operates in Kenya. My involvement was at the start; I am not on the team today, and that is fine, I was part of the inception and learned an enormous amount.

## The problem

Sanitation is a massive challenge in equatorial and developing regions. Conventional sewage systems are expensive to build and maintain, leaving millions without safe waste management.

## The approach

Use abundant solar energy to heat waste to pathogen-killing temperatures: a safe disposal method that does not depend on extensive infrastructure. During the Chile program we researched the water and sanitation sector, visited campamentos (informal settlements), built test units with Un Techo para Chile, tested pathogen elimination across temperatures and humidity, and prototyped the solar oven and container designs.

## What I took away

Technology alone is not enough; impact needs a business model that works within local economic realities, partnerships with communities rather than for them, and technical humility (simple, robust solutions over complex ones). These principles carried into every project since.

## Status

My involvement ended after the Chile program. The company continued in Naivasha, Kenya, and is [still operating today](https://sanivation.com/).
