# Nairobi Interns House (2008)

<!-- Photo slot: drop a file at /images/projects/nairobi-interns-house.jpg, then delete the comment markers on the next line. -->
<!-- <img src="/images/projects/nairobi-interns-house.jpg" width="100%" alt="The Nairobi interns house, 2008"> -->

In 2008 I came to Nairobi through an [AIESEC](https://aiesec.org/) internship placement with the University of Nairobi, sponsored by Kenya Commercial Bank (KCB), where I worked for six months. I organized weekly meetups for the other interns and set up a house where many of us lived together: Dutch, Chinese, German, and American. It was where I first realized that bringing people together and sharing stories is part of who I am.

This was the first of two Nairobi co-living houses. The second, with Kate, came in 2018-2019 (see [Nairobi Communal House]({{ '/projects/nairobi-house/' | prepend: site.baseurl }})).

## What I was testing

Whether a shared house and a regular gathering could turn a scattered group of interns into an actual community. It worked, and it set the pattern for [Adventure Report]({{ '/projects/adventure-report/' | prepend: site.baseurl }}), the [Friday gatherings]({{ '/projects/friday-gatherings/' | prepend: site.baseurl }}), and the later co-living house.

## The "be, do, have" thread

This period is also where I found the "be, do, have" framing that shaped how I have approached projects since.
